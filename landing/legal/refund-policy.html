Refund Policy

This policy explains when you can get money back from KiX. It applies to wallet top-ups, subscription fees and chargebacks, and supplements (does not replace) any mandatory consumer-protection rights you have under local law.

1. Scope

This policy applies to amounts paid by you to KiX (Singapore) Pte. Ltd. for use of the KiX platform — including wallet top-ups, subscription fees and any one-off charges. It does not cover payments made between a consumer and a merchant for the merchant’s underlying goods or services (for example, the price of a meal redeemed via a KiX offer); those disputes are between you and the merchant, although KiX may assist where it has visibility.

2. Wallet credit refunds

Unused wallet credit can be refunded within 14 days of the original top-up, provided:

  • The credit has not yet been spent on any campaign, acquisition, redemption or other paid feature.
  • The credit was not granted as a promotional bonus or matching grant (promotional credit is non-refundable and forfeits if the underlying paid credit is refunded).
  • The account is in good standing and not the subject of an active fraud or abuse investigation.

Refunds are returned to the original payment method via Stripe. Allow 5–10 business days for the funds to appear on your statement, depending on your bank.

For regions where local law mandates a different cooling-off period (for example, EU consumer law where applicable), we honour the longer of the local statutory window and the 14-day standard.

3. Spent advertising / acquisition spend

Amounts already spent on advertising, customer-acquisition events, redemptions, gamification placements or any other paid feature are non-refundable. This is consistent with industry practice for digital advertising: once an event has been delivered, the cost cannot be reversed. Where KiX is found, on investigation, to have charged for invalid traffic (bots, click fraud, internal traffic, mis-attribution) we will issue a credit to the merchant wallet equal to the amount in question; in exceptional cases we may refund to the original payment method.

4. Subscription fees

If a subscription plan is in place on your account:

  • Cancel any time from the merchant portal or by writing to billing@letskix.com.
  • If you cancel within 14 days of the first invoice and have not actively used the paid features, the subscription fee is refunded in full.
  • After 14 days, cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period and we issue a pro-rated refund for any whole calendar months that remain in the period if cancellation is for a documented service-affecting issue at KiX’s end.
  • Discounted annual plans are refundable only on a pro-rated basis less the discount applied at purchase.

5. How to request a refund

  1. Email billing@letskix.com from the email address on your account.
  2. Include the invoice number, the date of the original charge and the amount.
  3. Briefly describe the reason for the request.

We acknowledge requests within two business days and aim to decide within seven business days. Approved refunds are processed to the original payment method.

6. Disputes and escalation

If you disagree with a refund decision, you may escalate to legal@letskix.com with the original ticket number. We will conduct an internal review and respond in writing within 14 days. Nothing in this policy prevents you from pursuing the dispute through your local consumer-protection authority, the small-claims tribunal in your jurisdiction (for example, the Consumers Association of Singapore / Small Claims Tribunal), or — where the Terms of Service provide for it — SIAC arbitration.

7. Chargebacks

We strongly prefer to resolve disputes directly through the refund process in Sections 5 and 6 before any card chargeback. Where a chargeback is initiated:

  • Legitimate disputes (incorrect charge, duplicate, unauthorised use) are resolved in your favour and we will not contest.
  • “Friendly fraud” or fraudulent chargebacks — where the cardholder received the service and is using a chargeback to avoid payment — will be contested with evidence (login records, IP, redemption logs). Repeated abusive chargebacks may result in account suspension and forfeiture of any remaining wallet balance, subject to local law.
  • A chargeback fee equal to the amount charged to KiX by the card network (typically US$15–25) may be passed on where the chargeback is found to be unjustified, subject to local consumer-protection law.

9. Tax

Refunds include any tax actually paid on the refunded portion. Where tax has been remitted to a tax authority that does not permit a corresponding refund of the tax component, we refund only the net amount. The relevant tax credit, where available, will be reflected on a revised invoice.

9a. Worked examples

The following illustrative scenarios show how the policy applies. They are examples and not binding rulings on any specific case.

  • Top-up not used. A merchant tops up S$200 of wallet credit and decides three days later not to launch a campaign. Because none of the credit has been spent and the request falls within the 14-day window, KiX refunds S$200 to the original card. Bank settlement typically appears within 5–10 business days.
  • Top-up partly used. A merchant tops up S$200 and spends S$80 on placements before requesting a refund on day 10. KiX refunds the unused S$120; the S$80 already spent on delivered advertising events is non-refundable.
  • Promotional bonus. A merchant tops up S$200 and receives a S$50 promotional bonus. On day 5 the merchant requests a refund without having spent anything. KiX refunds the paid S$200; the S$50 promotional bonus is forfeited.
  • Invalid traffic credit. A merchant flags suspected bot traffic on a campaign. KiX investigates, confirms invalid traffic accounted for S$30 of charges, and issues a S$30 wallet credit. If the merchant prefers cash refund and we agree the issue was material, we may refund to the original card.
  • Annual subscription cancelled mid-term. A merchant pays S$1,200 for a 12-month plan (discounted from the monthly equivalent of S$120 × 12 = S$1,440) and cancels at month 7. The five remaining months are refunded on a pro-rated basis against the discounted price (S$1,200 × 5/12 = S$500), less any feature usage above the included quota.
  • Cooling-off (EU consumer). An EU-based consumer purchases a paid feature and asks to withdraw within 14 days. We refund in full where the user has not waived cooling-off by actively starting the service.

9b. What the policy does not cover

  • Underlying merchant transactions. If you bought a meal at a merchant using a KiX offer and the meal was unsatisfactory, the refund obligation lies with the merchant under the contract of sale, not KiX. We will pass on relevant evidence on request.
  • Third-party services. Where you reach a third-party service through KiX (for example, a partner integration), that service’s own refund terms apply.
  • Currency fluctuation. Where you topped up in one currency and the refund is settled in another, the FX rate is the rate at settlement; KiX does not compensate for FX losses.
  • Promotional credit. Credit granted as a promotional bonus is non-refundable and may expire under the terms of the promotion.

9c. Fraud and abuse safeguards

To protect honest users and merchants, refund requests are screened for indicators of abuse: high-velocity refund cycling, mismatched payment instruments, account-takeover signals or correlation with known fraud rings. Where we identify abuse, we may decline the request, hold processing pending verification, or reverse a previously granted refund. Decisions can be challenged via Section 6.

9d. Insolvency and service discontinuation

If KiX discontinues a service or undergoes corporate change, we will publish at least 30 days’ notice and process pending refund requests for unused balances under this policy. In the unlikely event of insolvency, unused wallet balances are treated as customer liabilities and handled in accordance with applicable insolvency law.

9e. Processing timelines and currency

Refunds are processed by our payment partner. Card refunds typically settle in 5–10 business days; bank-transfer refunds in selected regions can take up to 14 business days. Refunds are issued in the original currency of charge. Where the original payment method is no longer valid (expired card, closed bank account), we may issue refunds via an alternate method on verification of identity, in compliance with anti-money-laundering rules.

9f. Records and audit

We keep records of refund requests, decisions and processed amounts for the period required under applicable tax and accounting law (commonly five to seven years). Merchants can export refund records from the merchant portal at any time. Where local consumer-protection law obliges KiX to make refund decisions auditable on request, we will provide the relevant documentation to the supervisory authority.
