Acceptable Use Policy

KiX is a gamified merchant marketing platform. This policy describes what may and may not be uploaded, advertised or played on KiX, with extra rules for sensitive categories that vary by region. Violation of this policy is grounds for content removal, account suspension or termination under the Terms of Service.

Effective 2026-05-29 Applies to merchants, consumers, integrators

1. Prohibited content and conduct

The following are prohibited everywhere on KiX, regardless of region:

  • Real-money gambling. Casino-style games, sports betting, lottery, and games of pure chance offered for real-money stakes are prohibited. Skill-based promotional contests are permitted only where lawful and properly licensed.
  • Sexually explicit and adult content. Pornography, sexual services, escort services and content sexualising minors. (Sexualisation of minors is a zero-tolerance violation reported to authorities.)
  • Weapons. Firearms, ammunition, explosives, knives marketed for harm, and accessories that circumvent firearm regulation.
  • Drugs. Illegal drugs, controlled substances, prescription drugs offered without prescription, drug paraphernalia, and recreational drugs that are illegal in the target region.
  • Hate speech and harassment. Content that attacks, dehumanises or threatens people on protected grounds including race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, caste, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, age or serious disease.
  • Violent and graphic content. Glorification of violence, real-world gore, incitement, and content that promotes self-harm or suicide.
  • Intellectual-property infringement. Brand assets, trademarks, copyrighted media or counterfeit goods uploaded without the right to do so.
  • Deceptive practices. Misleading offers, bait-and-switch promotions, undisclosed material conditions, and reviews or testimonials that are not genuine.
  • Personal-data misuse. Scraping, harvesting or using KiX data outside the purposes permitted by the Terms and the Privacy Policy.
  • Malware and security abuse. Uploading malicious code, attempting to bypass security controls, or testing security without prior written authorisation.

Sensitive categories are gated by region. The platform’s compliance engine enforces minimum age, advertising-window and disclosure rules. Notable defaults:

Merchants are responsible for confirming the rules that apply to them in their target region before launching a campaign.

3. Platform abuse and integrity

  • No bots, scrapers, emulators or automation designed to inflate metrics, harvest data or evade rate limits.
  • No coordinated inauthentic behaviour (fake accounts, sockpuppets, vote brigading).
  • No interference with game integrity (cheating, tampering with client-side state to fake rewards).
  • No reverse-engineering of the platform beyond what mandatory local law permits.
  • No load testing or red-teaming against KiX infrastructure without prior written permission from security@letskix.com.

4. Enforcement: warnings, suspension, termination

Our enforcement is proportionate. For low-severity, first-time violations we typically issue a warning and request remediation within a stated time. Repeated, severe or harmful violations result in suspension or termination. Indicative scale:

5. Appeals process

  1. If your content is removed or your account is restricted, you will receive a notice in-product and by email.
  2. You may appeal within 30 days by replying to that notice or writing to trust@letskix.com with a brief written explanation.
  3. Appeals are reviewed by a different reviewer than the original decision-maker.
  4. We aim to decide appeals within 14 days. EU users have the right to refer disputes to a certified out-of-court dispute settlement body under DSA Art. 21.

6. Reporting violations

Anyone — user, merchant or third party — can report a violation:

  • In-product report button on offers, campaigns and merchant profiles.
  • Email trust@letskix.com. Include URLs / screenshots / merchant IDs where possible.
  • Reports of child sexual abuse material (CSAM) are escalated immediately and reported to NCMEC and applicable authorities in the relevant region.

7. Cooperation with law enforcement

KiX responds to valid legal process. We require an appropriate legal instrument from a competent authority and apply local law and applicable mutual-legal-assistance frameworks. Emergency requests where there is imminent risk to life or serious physical harm are reviewed urgently. We disclose only the minimum data necessary to respond to the request.

7a. Notice-and-action transparency

Where required by the EU Digital Services Act and similar transparency rules, we publish periodic reports on content moderation: volumes of notices received, actioned and overturned on appeal, average response times and the categories of content involved. The first report covering KiX alpha will be published once we have a representative reporting period.

  • Each enforcement action is logged with reason codes that map to this policy.
  • Notices to affected users contain the specific rule cited, the action taken, and a clear path to appeal.
  • Repeat-violator policies apply both to merchants whose campaigns are repeatedly flagged and to users who repeatedly post disallowed content.

7b. Worked examples

The following examples are illustrative and not binding rulings on any specific case.

  • Alcohol creative in Singapore. A merchant uploads a game that features an alcoholic drink prominently in a child-friendly art style. The compliance engine flags the asset; the campaign is paused; the merchant is asked to either age-gate the audience and adjust creative, or remove the alcohol element. Low-severity, remediation-first.
  • Halal claim without certification. A merchant in Indonesia advertises a product as halal but cannot produce certification details. The campaign is paused; the merchant is asked to upload the certificate or remove the claim.
  • Bot inflation. An integrator drives obviously automated traffic to inflate metrics. KiX revokes the integrator’s API keys and may reverse any charges or commissions tied to invalid traffic.
  • IP infringement. A merchant uploads a logo that is the registered trademark of a non-affiliated brand. The asset is removed on notice; repeated infringements lead to account termination.
  • EU minor-targeted advertising. A merchant configures targeting that, in combination with profiling signals, would direct an ad to a known minor in the EU. The campaign cannot launch until targeting is corrected (DSA Art. 28).
  • Hate speech in game copy. A campaign’s tagline disparages a religious group. The content is removed immediately; the merchant is suspended pending review and may be terminated for severity.

7c. Repeat-violator framework

  • Three medium-severity actions within 90 days escalates the next medium action to high.
  • Two high-severity actions within 12 months escalates the next high action to termination.
  • Critical-severity actions bypass progressive enforcement.
  • Appeals that succeed do not count against the violator’s record.

7d. Consumer-side conduct

Consumers using the KiX app are bound by the same policy. In particular, consumers must not:

  • Use bots, emulators or modified clients to play games or claim rewards.
  • Operate multiple accounts to circumvent per-user reward limits.
  • Harass merchants or other users through reviews, reports or messaging features.
  • Submit false reports to weaponise the abuse-reporting system.

7e. Brand-asset rules

Brand assets uploaded into KiX games are inserted at designated asset slots in each game template. Merchants must ensure that:

  • The asset is owned by the merchant or used with a valid licence.
  • The asset is suitable for the audience the campaign will be served to (taking into account age, region and sensitive-category rules).
  • The asset does not contain hidden tracking, malware or steganographic payloads.
  • The asset respects the dimensions and content guidance documented in the slot specification — overlays that obscure required disclosures or that break the game’s mechanics are not permitted.

Where an asset is reused across regions, the strictest applicable rule governs. KiX may remove or replace assets that do not match the slot specification, with notice to the merchant.

7f. Halal, kosher and dietary claims

Where a campaign or asset claims a dietary certification (halal, kosher, vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, etc.), the merchant must hold and be able to produce the certification on request. False or expired claims are treated as deceptive practices under Section 1. In Muslim-majority targeting in Indonesia and Malaysia, halal claims and corresponding documentation are mandatory for the categories specified by national authorities.
