# KiX Competitor Learning Report — Wave Trinity
**Date:** 2026-05-31
**Author:** Competitive analysis (Claude, web-sourced)
**Scope:** Playable, Flarie, CataBoom, BRAME, Gamify (5 most relevant of 9 named)
**Methodology:** WebSearch + direct WebFetch of homepages & pricing pages. Every quantitative claim is either cited or marked "unverified".

---

## TL;DR

All 5 competitors are **template + drag-and-drop** gamification SaaS for **marketing/CRM teams at brands**, not for **merchants themselves**. None of them (verified as of 2026-05-31) ship an **AI-generated game builder** as the headline product — closest is Playable Factory (a separate company, not Playable.com) doing AI playable *ads*. None of them position around the **5-step merchant funnel** (Game → Reward → Register → Redeem → Return). KiX's wedge — *self-serve for offline merchants, AI-generated games, store-redemption loop* — is genuinely unoccupied. But these competitors are **5–15 years ahead on trust signals**: named blue-chip logos (KFC, Heineken, Chipotle, Carlsberg, Klarna), published pricing tiers, case-study metrics, and an integration story KiX still lacks.

## 1. Per-Competitor Cards

### 1.1 Playable (Denmark) — playable.com

- **Positioning:** "Where play means business — the gamification platform for marketers." Brand/CRM marketers, not merchants. ([playable.com](https://playable.com/))
- **Pricing:** 3 tiers — Essentials / Core / Premium. All "Talk to us" — **no public price**. Unlimited campaigns at every tier; differentiator is integrations depth (Standard → Web-hook → API) + security/account structure. ([playable.com/pricing](https://playable.com/pricing/))
- **Named customers:** Virgin Red, Carlsberg, Costcutter, PSV Eindhoven, Icelandair, Arla. Claims "650+ brands globally, 15 games played per second" (latter is unverified marketing copy).
- **Mobile-first UX maturity:** 40+ game types, "built-in integrations." Visual quality of games unverified without playing them; case studies focus on CPA/redemption metrics, not visual craft.
- **AI story:** **None advertised on homepage** (verified 2026-05-31). Note: Playable*Factory* (different company, Berat Oguz) ships AI-generated playable *ads* — easy to confuse, distinct product. ([pocketgamer.biz](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/beyond-the-guesswork-introducing-playable-factorys-ai-generated-playables/))
- **Integrations:** Claims "50+ native integrations" (CRM, marketing automation, ESP, analytics) per Capterra summary. Specific partner names not on homepage. ([capterra.com Playable](https://www.capterra.com/p/179977/Playable/))
- **Case-study format:** Single-metric hero ("2x redemption", "lowest CPA in company history") — emotionally punchy, easy to copy.
- **Learn-from:** (a) Three-tier laddered onboarding language (Essentials / Core / Premium); (b) one-line metric case studies; (c) "650+ brands" social-proof bar.

### 1.2 Flarie (Sweden) — flarie.com

- **Positioning:** "Premium Branded Games for Marketing and CRM teams looking to level up their engagement." Squarely brand-marketer, not merchant. ([flarie.com](https://flarie.com/))
- **Pricing:** 4 tiers — Demo / Launch Pad / Sky / Space. Specific numbers **not published**. Demo includes 14-day trial; tiers differentiate by user seats (1 / 1 / 5 / 10) and Gamification API access at top. ([flarie.com/pricing](https://flarie.com/pricing))
- **Named customers (verified, with metric):**
  - Garnier — 3x CTR on social
  - Ingo — +20% sales
  - Klarna — +350% time-in-app
  - Cathay Pacific — 6.6x newsletter CTR
  - Also: Kärcher, Henkel, Utility Warehouse, Hot Head Burritos, Fairtrade, Schär, Tibber, Pågen, Mecenat
- **Mobile-first UX maturity:** 160+ games across arcade/puzzle/sports/chance per Capterra. Premium positioning suggests strong visual craft (unverified without play-through).
- **AI story:** **None advertised** on homepage as of 2026-05-31.
- **Integrations:** URL/iframe-first (any channel). Webhooks at Sky tier, Gamification API at Space tier. No named partners.
- **Learn-from:** (a) The **"premium" positioning** as differentiator vs. cheap-template competitors — they own the high end; (b) **paired metric callouts** (brand + single bold % lift) on hero; (c) tier names with metaphorical ladder ("Launch Pad → Sky → Space") — memorable.

### 1.3 CataBoom (US) — cataboom.com

- **Positioning:** "Make Your Loyalty Program Fun with Gamification." Targets QSR + retail loyalty teams. ([cataboom.com](https://www.cataboom.com/))
- **Pricing — fully public (RARE in this space, very valuable benchmark):** ([cataboom.com/pricing](https://www.cataboom.com/pricing))
  - **Catapult X** — $500/month, month-to-month, 1 active campaign
  - **Annual plans starting at $12,000/year**
  - Essentials / Core / Advanced / Enterprise — differentiate on game-library depth (chance only → +skill → +Collect & Win / Punch Card → full library) and concurrent active campaigns (1 / 2 / 4 / unlimited)
- **Named QSR customers:** Chipotle, Taco Bell, KFC, CKE Restaurants, Whataburger. Plus Amazon, Southwest, T-Mobile, Michaels, Dell, Foot Locker. This is the **strongest QSR roster of all 5**.
- **Mobile-first UX maturity:** 200+ game mechanic options across 9 modules. Visual maturity unverified.
- **AI story:** **None.** Explicitly template-first, "no coding required."
- **Integrations:** Vague — "5 deployment methods" and "multiple touch points." No named POS / CRM partners. **This is a weak spot.**
- **Differentiators bundled with every tier:** "Fraud and bot mitigation" + "Dynamic pricing engine" — sophisticated and worth noting.
- **Learn-from:** (a) **Publish prices.** $500/mo entry point legitimizes the whole category and gives prospects a clear ladder; (b) **Active-campaign cap as price lever** (1 → 2 → 4 → unlimited) — clean, defensible; (c) bundling fraud/bot mitigation at every tier as a baseline trust signal.

### 1.4 BRAME (Switzerland) — brame.io

- **Positioning:** "Drag and drop your way to Gamification Marketing campaigns that convert, retain, engage." ([brame.io](https://www.brame.io/))
- **Pricing — fully public with multi-currency:** ([brame.io/pricing](https://www.brame.io/pricing))
  - **Light:** €440/mo (€5,280/yr) + €525 setup, 1 user, 3 campaigns, 1 live, 20+ templates
  - **Essentials:** €880/mo (€10,560/yr) + €1,060 setup, unlimited campaigns, 3 live, 30+ templates, CRM
  - **Business:** custom, 3 users, unlimited live, 50+ templates, tracking pixels
  - **Enterprise:** custom, unlimited users, 100+ templates, loyalty program integration
  - Also publishes GBP and USD prices alongside EUR.
- **Named customers (strongest enterprise roster of all 5):** KFC, Heineken, Lindt, REWE, OBI, SPAR, Nespresso, AEG, McDonald's, BIPA, Tchibo, Papa Johns, Motel One, S.Oliver. SPAR Switzerland has a dedicated case study. ([brame.io SPAR case](https://www.brame.io/customer-stories/loyalty-reloaded-how-spar-switzerland-innovated-customer-engagement-through-gamification))
- **Mobile-first UX maturity:** Drag-and-drop builder, no-code. Unverified visual depth.
- **AI story:** **None.**
- **Integrations:** "Easy integration into your marketing tech stack" + iframe distribution. Loyalty program integration only at Enterprise tier. No named partners on homepage.
- **Learn-from:** (a) **Multi-currency price display** (EUR / GBP / USD side-by-side) — kills the "is this for me?" friction for international prospects; (b) **Setup fee as a separate line item** — anchors enterprise expectations without inflating monthly; (c) explicitly **count templates per tier** (20 / 30 / 50 / 100) — concrete value ladder.

### 1.5 Gamify (Australia) — gamify.com

- **Positioning:** "Play meets purpose. Gamified experiences that drive impact." Agency-flavored — works *through* media agencies (99, True, OMD) rather than direct. ([gamify.com](https://www.gamify.com/))
- **Pricing:** **Not disclosed.** Project / managed-service feel.
- **Named customers (verified, with metrics):** ([gamify.com/case-studies](https://www.gamify.com/case-studies))
  - **KFC Japan** — 850K+ plays, 600+ hours playtime, **22% in-store voucher redemption, +106% store sales YoY**
  - **Donut Papi (Sydney)** — +581% organic social shares & email DB growth (SMB case study!)
  - Wendy's, Nissan, ResMed, Banana Boat, Red Balloon, Pita Pit, V Energy
- **Mobile-first UX maturity:** Proprietary game engine; powers gamification widgets on **Wix.com** (per their claim — unverified but plausible; Wix has 100M+ users).
- **AI story:** Mentions "automated winner management" only. No AI generation.
- **Integrations:** Wix, Shopify, custom apps. Agency partner network (OMD etc.).
- **Learn-from:** (a) **KFC Japan 22% in-store redemption** is the single best published merchant-funnel metric we found — this is essentially proof KiX's funnel works at scale; we should chase a parallel case study; (b) the **Donut Papi small-merchant case** is a gold template for KiX (single F&B SMB + measurable lift) — copy this format verbatim; (c) **agency-partner GTM** is a real distribution channel we shouldn't ignore.

## 3. Things We Should COPY or STEAL

### 3.1 Publish entry-tier pricing — CataBoom's $500/mo move
CataBoom is the **only** competitor publishing a real entry price ($500/mo month-to-month). This anchors the category, builds trust, and lets us position KiX *below* or *at* this number with "AI generation included" as the differentiator. BRAME also publishes (€440), reinforcing that ~$450–500/mo is the public anchor. **Action:** publish a "Merchant Starter" tier at $99–$199/mo (AI-native cost structure lets us undercut) before launch.

### 3.2 BRAME's multi-currency, multi-template-count tiering
BRAME shows EUR/GBP/USD side-by-side and counts templates per tier (20 / 30 / 50 / 100). For KiX's 200-country first-mover ambition, copy this exactly with localized currency and "AI games/month included" as the analogous lever (e.g., 5 / 25 / 100 / unlimited). Setup fee as separate line is also smart enterprise framing.

### 3.3 Flarie's paired-metric hero pattern
"Garnier — 3x CTR on social. Klarna — +350% time in app." One brand + one bold number. Currently the cleanest case-study format in the category. **Action:** every KiX merchant case study should be one line: "Xiao Wang's Coffee — 47% return-visit lift, 28 days." No paragraphs.

### 3.4 Gamify's "small-merchant + big-number" case format (Donut Papi)
The Donut Papi case (+581% organic share / email DB growth, single Sydney shop) is the **exact template** for KiX's storytelling. Every competitor except Gamify lives in mid-market enterprise. We can own the SMB story they all neglect. **Action:** prioritize 3 lighthouse SMB cases from city-10-merchant rollout, each with one bold metric.

### 3.5 CataBoom's "bundled trust at every tier" — fraud/bot mitigation
CataBoom bundles fraud + bot mitigation in every plan. For self-serve merchants who fear "AI games will be gamed," this is a critical trust signal. **Action:** ship a "Merchant Protection" suite (bot detection, redemption-fraud guard, one-redemption-per-phone) and bundle it at every tier from day 1.

### 3.6 (Bonus) Tier metaphor ladders
Flarie's "Launchpad → Sky → Space" and CataBoom's "Catapult X" are memorable. Avoid generic Basic/Pro/Enterprise. Consider a KiX-native ladder tied to the funnel (e.g., "Game / Reward / Redeem / Return" or city-scale "Stall / Street / District / City").

## 4. Things They Do WORSE Than Us (Double Down Here)

### 4.1 None of them ship AI-generated games
Verified on all 5 homepages (2026-05-31). Closest is Playable*Factory* (different company; AI playable *ads* not full games). KiX's brick pipeline + ELTM + Coder guardrails is genuinely category-creating. **Double down:** "AI-generated, brand-customized in 60 seconds" should be the hero — copy that none of them can match without rebuilding from scratch.

### 4.2 All 5 target brand marketers, not merchants
Every single competitor's hero copy says "marketing teams" / "CRM teams" / "brand managers." The offline merchant is invisible to them. KiX's "Xiao Wang the F&B store owner" persona is unoccupied territory. **Double down:** make the merchant the hero of every screen — pricing, copy, dashboards. They will not pivot here easily; their entire sales motion is enterprise.

### 4.3 None close the redemption loop in-store
CataBoom is closest (loyalty-program amplifier) but still hands off to the brand's loyalty stack. Nobody publishes a Register→Redeem→Return funnel. KiX's 5-step funnel — especially Redeem (in-store) → Return (repeat visit) — is the **defensible piece**. **Double down:** publish the funnel-conversion benchmarks per step (% of game plays that convert to registration, redemption, return) — make this the industry metric we own.

### 4.4 Self-serve is paywalled or absent
Playable: "Talk to us." Flarie: "Book a demo." Gamify: agency project. BRAME: setup fee + 14-day onboarding. CataBoom is the only true self-serve at $500/mo. KiX can be the **first true self-serve at SMB price** — sign up, generate, deploy in 10 minutes, no sales call. **Double down:** "Live in 10 minutes, no sales call" should be a homepage promise.

### 4.5 Visual craft is template-bounded
Every competitor brags about template counts (40, 100, 160, 200+). Templates are by definition stale and same-y. KiX's brick + asset-slot brand injection + PDCA visual polish produces *bespoke per merchant*. **Double down:** show side-by-side: "Their template after brand swap" vs "KiX game generated for your store." This is the demo that closes.

## 5. 12-Month Prediction: Who Copies Us Back, and How to Defend

**Most likely copier: BRAME** (followed closely by Playable).

**Why BRAME:**
- Already publishes EUR/GBP/USD pricing — has enterprise infrastructure for new tier launches.
- McDonald's + KFC + SPAR roster — already adjacent to QSR / merchant.
- Swiss engineering org — fastest to bolt on an "AI Game Generator" module to an existing drag-and-drop builder.
- Has Loyalty Program integration at Enterprise — closest competitor to KiX's redemption loop.

**What they'll copy:** an "AI-generate this template variant" button inside their existing drag-and-drop builder. They'll *not* rebuild the engine — they'll bolt LLM prompt-to-template-tweak onto existing assets. Marketing line will be "AI-assisted gamification builder."

**Why this is a weaker copy:** their building block is still the *template*. KiX builds from a *brick definition* (PRD + wireframe + rules + contracts) — a fundamentally different abstraction. Their AI feature will be cosmetic (color/copy tweak), ours is structural (game logic + brand-fit + visual polish).

**Defense plan:**

1. **Lock in 10 merchants per city × 200 countries (the speed-and-lockin moat from internal memory).** This is the real moat — not the AI tech. Once Xiao Wang has 6 KiX games live with redemption tracking, switching to BRAME's bolt-on AI = throwing away history.
2. **Publish the funnel metric benchmark before they do.** Own "Game→Redeem conversion %" as the industry-standard KPI. Competitors who lack the redemption loop can't even measure it.
3. **Open the brick library + sample_brander as a developer ecosystem.** If 3rd parties contribute bricks, BRAME's bolt-on AI can't keep up with breadth. (Mirror of Shopify-vs-incumbents 2010.)
4. **Patent or trade-secret the PDCA visual-polish pipeline.** The brick → ELTM → Coder → PDCA loop with property-oracle gating is the hardest part to clone — it took 6 months of iteration with documented failure modes. Don't open-source this piece.
5. **Pre-empt the "AI gamification" SEO category.** Buy / rank for "AI gamification platform," "AI marketing games for merchants," "AI loyalty game generator" before BRAME's press release.

**Secondary risk: Playable.** They have the brand roster (Carlsberg, Virgin Red) and Danish design DNA. If they acquire or partner with Playable Factory (the AI playable ads company with same-root name), they instantly have an AI generation story. Watch for an M&A announcement — that's the trigger.
